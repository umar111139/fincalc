Frequently Asked Questions

What is EMI and how is it calculated? ▼
EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment made to a lender on a set date each month. The formula is: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n ÷ [(1+r)^n − 1] where P = principal, r = monthly interest rate, and n = total number of months.

What is the difference between EMI and mortgage? ▼
Both are loan repayment structures. EMI is a general term used for any loan repayment. Mortgage specifically refers to home loans with property as collateral. Our mortgage calculator goes further by adding property insurance, taxes, HOA fees, and PMI for a complete monthly cost picture.
